# Project Overview

> Compiled only from the contest CSV submission and the team's uploaded Business Model / Project Report files. No external judgment or invented claims.

## Short Description (from submission form)

MediRide is a smart emergency healthcare platform that connects patients with the nearest available ambulance through real-time GPS tracking, ensuring faster and more reliable emergency response. The platform eliminates the hassle of manually searching for ambulances during critical situations by enabling one-tap SOS requests, live ambulance tracking, transparent pricing, and direct communication with drivers. Beyond ambulance services, MediRide offers blood donor matching, hospital and doctor information, post-emergency follow-up, event medical coverage, and a web-based emergency access system for users without the mobile app. By leveraging digital technology and strategic partnerships with ambulance providers and healthcare institutions, MediRide aims to improve emergency healthcare accessibility, reduce response time, and save lives across Bangladesh while building an efficient and transparent emergency transportation ecosystem

## Product
**MediRide** — app connecting patients to nearest ambulance in real time; SOS; live tracking; post-emergency follow-up; event coverage; also blood donor finder, hospital/doctor info, appointment booking (to drive non-emergency app use); web access for users without the app.

## Problem (Project Report)
Patients struggle to find a reliable ambulance quickly in emergencies.

## Existing alternatives named (Lean Canvas)
Shamim Ambulance, 24 Ambulance, sheba.xyz, local ambulance drivers, hospital ambulance systems.

## Revenue model (Business Model)
- **15% commission** on every completed ambulance trip (owner keeps 85%)
- Event medical coverage service fees
- Referral commission from hospitals/diagnostics
- Corporate healthcare contracts
- Future: premium subscriptions for ambulance owners; featured listings/ads

## Cost structure (Business Model)
Technology (app/web/cloud/APIs); operations (support, driver management); marketing; administration (legal/compliance/partnerships).

## Go-to-market (Business Model)
Phase 1: partner with ambulance associations; pilot in Rajshahi. Phase 2: hospitals + awareness campaigns.

## Channels / segments (Lean Canvas & form)
Social, push, influencer, app/website, billboards; early adopters tech-adapted, ~20–35; doctors, engineers, bankers, govt employees / 1st class officers.
