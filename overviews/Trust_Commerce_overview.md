# Project Overview

> Compiled only from the contest CSV submission and the team's uploaded Business Model & Project Report files. No external judgment or invented claims.

## Short Description (from submission form)

TrustCommerce is an escrow-based social e-commerce and payment platform where a seller lists what they're selling (a product, service, contract, or even a bet/agreement), a buyer agrees and pays the amount into TrustCommerce instead of directly to the seller. TrustPay holds the funds until the buyer confirms they received what was promised. If a dispute arises, TrustPay's review team investigates (chat logs, proof of delivery, photos/videos, etc.) and releases the money to whichever party is in the right. It combines marketplace + payment gateway + dispute resolution in one platform, and supports withdrawals/deposits via bKash, Nagad, Rocket, Upay, cards, and bank transfer.

## Product

**Trust Commerce / TrustCommerce** — social e-commerce + escrow-based payment platform for safe online buying/selling and contracts. Sellers get a personal storefront/showcasing website; buyers pay into escrow (not directly to the seller); funds release on confirmation or after dispute review.

## Problem (from Project Report)

Two-sided trust deficit in online commerce (esp. Bangladesh-like markets):
- Buyers pay upfront and risk wrong/damaged/missing goods with weak recourse
- Sellers ship or complete work and risk non-payment or false non-delivery claims
- No neutral, fast dispute mediator
- SSL / online payment verification is complex for new sellers
- New sellers struggle to create a website or payment account

## Proposed flow (from Project Report)

1. Seller creates profile and lists product/service/agreement with price and terms  
2. Buyer pays into TrustCommerce escrow wallet (not to seller)  
3. Seller delivers / fulfills  
4. Buyer confirms  
5. On confirm → funds released to seller  
6. On dispute → review team uses chat logs, delivery proof, photos, tracking → awards funds  
7. Seller withdraws via bKash, Nagad, Rocket, Upay, card, or bank transfer  

## Target audience (Report + form)

- Individual online buyers/sellers including Facebook/Instagram shops  
- SMEs without trust/escrow infrastructure  
- P2P / used goods / local classifieds  
- Freelancers & clients (milestone payments)

## Business model (Project Report + Canvas OCR)

| Revenue stream | Description (as stated) |
|----------------|-------------------------|
| Transaction fee | 2–5% per completed escrow transaction (seller, buyer, or split) |
| Withdrawal fee | Fee when cashing out to MFS/bank/card |
| Premium seller accounts | Verified badges, lower fees, priority dispute resolution |
| Escrow-as-a-Service (API) | License escrow engine to other e-commerce platforms |
| Priority dispute resolution | Optional expedited review fee |

**Canvas (OCR):** Key partners include payment processors / MFS; key activities: escrow management, storefront/content tools, dispute resolution, payment processing; tagline: “Building Trust in Every Transaction and Interaction.” Cost structure: payment gateway fees, dispute team salaries, tech/hosting, marketing.

## Key features (Project Report)

- Escrow wallet with automated hold-and-release  
- Buyer/seller profiles with reputation/rating  
- Internal dispute-resolution dashboard  
- Multi-channel payouts: bKash, Nagad, Rocket, Upay, cards, banks  
- Flexible use cases: products, services, informal agreements  
- In-platform chat and evidence upload  

## Challenges & mitigation (Project Report)

| Challenge | Mitigation stated |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Manual dispute review may not scale | Tiered review (auto for simple, human for complex) |
| Regulatory compliance for holding funds | Partner with licensed aggregators/MFS; pursue licensing early |
| Collusion / fake disputes | Reputation system + pattern detection |
| Cash flow while holding escrow | Segregated escrow accounts; conservative float management |

## Roadmap (Project Report)

1. Core escrow marketplace + manual dispute resolution  
2. Full MFS + card/bank rails  
3. Seller reputation scoring + semi-automated dispute triage  
4. Escrow-as-a-Service API  
5. Regional expansion to similar trust-gap markets
