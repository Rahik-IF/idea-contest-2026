## Short Description (from submission form)

Solar Snow is a startup project developed by students of Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET). The project aims to address the significant post-harvest losses faced by small-scale farmers in Bangladesh due to inadequate cold storage facilities. Its mission is to provide affordable, portable, and solar-powered cold storage solutions that help farmers preserve vegetables and fruits, reduce spoilage, and increase their income. 

The team identified that conventional cold storage systems are often expensive, fixed in location, dependent on grid electricity or diesel, and mainly focused on potato storage. These limitations make them inaccessible to many rural farmers. To solve this problem, Solar Snow proposes a portable cold storage container powered by solar energy and equipped with batteries, insulation, cooling units, IoT devices, and smart monitoring systems. 

A key feature of the solution is its off-grid capability and portability, allowing it to operate in remote agricultural areas without reliable electricity. The final design includes a preservation chamber, cooler chamber, control room, and a smartphone application that provides real-time monitoring and control through IoT technology. The system is designed to store up to 4,000 kg of agricultural products while maintaining appropriate temperature conditions for different commodities. 

The project expects to create substantial social and environmental impacts. According to the presentation, Solar Snow can extend the shelf life of perishable foods from around 2 days to up to 21 days, reduce post-harvest losses by approximately 80%, and increase farmers' annual income by about 25%. Additionally, the use of solar energy helps reduce dependence on diesel-powered systems and supports carbon emission reduction. 

To ensure commercial viability, Solar Snow offers multiple business models including direct purchase, rental, and community-sharing options, making the technology accessible to farmers with different financial capacities. Financial projections indicate a payback period of around 3.5 years, while market analysis shows strong growth potential in the global cold storage sector. 

The team behind Solar Snow has already achieved significant recognition, including positions in national innovation and climate competitions. Their long-term plan focuses on product development, pilot deployment, market expansion, farmer training, and strategic partnerships to scale the solution across Bangladesh and beyond. Overall, Solar Snow presents an innovative and sustainable approach to reducing food waste and improving the livelihoods of farmers through renewable-energy-powered cold storage technology.
