## Short Description (from submission form)

KrishiOS is an AI and IoT-powered smart agriculture platform designed to help farmers make data-driven decisions throughout the entire farming lifecycle. It combines artificial intelligence, Internet of Things (IoT), weather information, computer vision, and farm management tools into a single, easy-to-use platform.

The system enables farmers to monitor crops, identify plant diseases through image analysis, receive personalized cultivation recommendations, optimize irrigation and fertilizer usage, track farming expenses and production, estimate profits, and access real-time weather and market insights. By analyzing data from IoT sensors and AI models, KrishiOS provides timely recommendations that help reduce crop loss, improve productivity, lower production costs, and increase farmers' income.

To make the platform accessible to all farmers, including those without smartphones or internet access, KrishiOS can also support an IVR (Interactive Voice Response) service, allowing users to receive agricultural advice through simple phone calls in their native language.

## Paper abstract highlights (Project Report)
Context: >60% smallholders manual practices; 40–50% avoidable water waste cited; Bangladesh >87% smallholders; unreliable grid/connectivity. Dual-mode crop & aquaculture; off-grid; voice/SMS alerts.

## Requirements (Methodology)
(i) Operate without grid/internet; (ii) alerts to feature phone with no data plan; (iii) BOM **BDT 8,000–12,000** (~USD 70–110).

## Prototype results (Paper)
KrishiOS v1.0 lab prototype: ESP32, DHT11, moisture sensor, etc.; 72-hour bench trial; 5-minute logging. Mentions MobileNetV2/EfficientNet-Lite on ESP32-S3 for offline disease detection; 20 W power architecture in conclusion.
