## Project snapshot (from submissions)

Solar Snow is a portable solar-powered cold storage container for small-scale farmers: panels, batteries, cooling, insulation, IoT/app monitoring. Mission: cut post-harvest spoilage and raise income off-grid. Prototype BOM example ~৳70,000; claimed final capacity 4000 kg/unit, 6 kW cooling, shelf life 2→21 days. Commercial models: purchase ৳17,61,400; rental ৳4,860/week; community ৳850/person/week (6 farmers/unit).

---

## 1. Innovation & Originality — 3.5/5

### What was evaluated
Novelty of portable shared solar cold storage versus known cold-chain solutions.

### Analysis
Solar refrigeration and cold rooms are known; Solar Snow’s differentiation is **portability + three access models** (own, rent, community share). That packaging is thoughtful applied innovation for smallholders locked out of potato-centric, non-portable, diesel/grid stores. Not a new physics breakthrough—solid productization originality.

### Why this score
**3.5/5** — Meaningful applied packaging; core tech is established.

---

## 2. Feasibility — 3/5

### What was evaluated
Credibility of prototype vs commercial pricing and off-grid performance claims.

### Analysis
Prototype BOM (~৳70k) and component list are tangible. Jump to ৳17.61L purchase price and 4000 kg / 6 kW final product needs much more engineering and cost validation. Community/rental pricing is more approachable. Off-grid thermal performance, maintenance, and logistics of moving containers are hard. Feasibility is medium: direction clear, scale-up gap large.

### Why this score
**3/5** — Prototype evidence exists; commercial scale and price credibility are weak points.

---

## 3. Impact & Relevance — 5/5

### What was evaluated
Post-harvest loss and smallholder income—central BD agri problems.

### Analysis
Food spoilage, unstable markets, and reduced farmer income are explicitly framed; primary users are small-scale farmers. If portable cold storage works, impact on waste and livelihoods is transformative. Among entries, this ties Dishari/KrishiOS for raw development relevance.

### Why this score
**5/5** — Direct attack on a top-tier national agri/food-loss problem.
