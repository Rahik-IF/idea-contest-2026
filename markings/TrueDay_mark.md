## Project snapshot (from submissions)

TrueDay replaces traditional interviews with AI-generated 3-day work simulations built from a company’s real tools (Slack, Jira, GitHub, Figma, Google Workspace). Hiring managers receive a Moment Replay of critical decisions; candidates receive a Personal Debrief. Behavioral KPIs include response latency, selective attention under dual pressure, and communication shifts. SaaS pricing: Starter $199 / Growth $499 / Scale $999 per month.

---

## 1. Innovation & Originality — 4.5/5

### What was evaluated
Whether the hiring product is a meaningful new mechanism versus generic AI interview bots.

### Analysis
The core insight—companies hire interview performers, not job performers—is well known; TrueDay’s **mechanism** is not. Auto-generating a multi-day simulation from live company context, then scoring invisible behaviors (latency, dual-pressure prioritization, tab-switching, communication under load) and delivering Moment Replay rather than a single score is a distinctive product design. It is closer to a “work sample at scale” than to ChatGPT mock interviews.

Live URL (`true-day.vercel.app`) and crisp pricing narrative (“priced like software, valued like hiring insurance”) reinforce that this is a productized idea, not a slide-only concept. Relative to SkillMatch and other career-AI entries, originality is clearly higher.

### Why this score
**4.5/5** — Sharp, differentiated product mechanism; just short of 5 because the broader “AI hiring” space exists, but the 3-day simulation + replay design is standout in this cohort.

## 2. Feasibility — 4/5

### What was evaluated
Buildability, go-to-market clarity, and execution risk for a student SaaS.

### Analysis
Software-only scope, clear user flow (connect tools → generate scenario → candidate link → replay/debrief), and published pricing tiers make this one of the more executable ideas. A live deployment is cited. Main risks are enterprise trust (reading Slack/Jira), simulation quality, and sales cycles—not whether an MVP can exist. Compared with hardware or multi-sided emergency platforms, feasibility is strong.

### Why this score
**4/5** — Credible MVP and coherent SaaS BM; enterprise integration/trust keeps it from a perfect 5.

## 3. Impact & Relevance — 3.5/5

### What was evaluated
Who benefits and how large the social/economic effect is in the contest’s BD/RUET context.

### Analysis
Bad-hire cost ($17k, 74% admit bad hires—as cited in slides) matters to startups and tech employers. Impact is real but **narrow**: founders, CTOs, HR, recruiters—not mass citizen welfare. It does not address the same scale of harm as post-harvest loss, emergency delay, or BVI mobility. Strong B2B relevance; moderate “Impact & Relevance” versus public-good heavyweights.

### Why this score
**3.5/5** — Clear organizational value; limited population and social-crisis reach versus top impact scorers.
