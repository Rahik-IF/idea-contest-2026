## Project snapshot (from submissions)

TreeLease lets urban customers reserve the seasonal harvest of a specific mango tree from a registered orchard, with delivery or orchard pickup. Farmers get advance payment and market access; consumers get traceable fruit. Revenue: reservation fees, booking commission, delivery charges, premium visits. Segments include urban families, enthusiasts, corporate gifting, restaurants/cafés, and expatriates.

---

## 1. Innovation & Originality — 4/5

### What was evaluated
Business-concept originality in the local agri-commerce context (not deep-tech novelty).

### Analysis
“Rent a named mango tree for a season” is a memorable, culturally fitted idea for Bangladesh. It is not ML research, but originality in **market design**—tree-level reservation, traceability, advance farmer payment—is high among non-tech-heavy entries. It differs from generic farm marketplaces by making the tree the SKU. That conceptual clarity earns a strong innovation mark for a business idea contest.

### Why this score
**4/5** — Fresh local business concept; not deep tech, so not a 5 against Dishari/CryptoSilicon-style invention.

---

## 2. Feasibility — 4/5

### What was evaluated
Operational doability of orchard partnerships, bookings, payments, and seasonal logistics.

### Analysis
No exotic hardware; BM lists partners (orchards, delivery, payments, agri orgs), activities, and costs clearly. Student teams can build a booking platform. Hard parts are seasonal trust, quality control, delivery of perishable mangoes, and onboarding enough orchards—real but ordinary marketplace risks. Compared with hover vehicles or silicon IP, feasibility is high.

### Why this score
**4/5** — Scope matches a student venture; seasonal ops risk prevents a 5.

---

## 3. Impact & Relevance — 3.5/5

### What was evaluated
Livelihood and consumer benefit versus national-scale problem severity.

### Analysis
Advance payment and better market access for orchard owners plus provenance for urban buyers are meaningful. Impact is seasonal and niche (mango), not a year-round critical system. Strong agri-commerce relevance; lower than cold storage, emergency, or assistive mobility on raw human-stakes.

### Why this score
**3.5/5** — Real farmer/consumer value; limited by seasonality and niche scope.
