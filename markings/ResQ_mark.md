## Project snapshot (from submissions)

ResQ Plus is an AI coordination layer linking citizens, volunteers, hospitals, ambulances, and families. MVP: Smart SOS, volunteer network, AI triage assistant, family alerts, live GPS, hospital recommendation. Monetization is multi-stakeholder (gov licensing, hospital subscriptions, NGO sponsorship, corporate safety, insurance, premium consumer).

---

## 1. Innovation & Originality — 3.5/5

### What was evaluated
Whether “coordination layer” is more than a generic SOS app.

### Analysis
The slogan “services exist; coordination doesn’t” is a useful framing. Feature set is coherent systems integration rather than a single novel algorithm. Better than a bare SOS clone; not as original as Dishari’s tactile architecture or TrueDay’s simulation mechanic.

### Why this score
**3.5/5** — Solid systems concept; integration-heavy rather than invention-heavy.

---

## 2. Feasibility — 2.5/5

### What was evaluated
Cold-start and multi-sided marketplace difficulty.

### Analysis
Needs citizens + volunteers + hospitals + ambulances at once. Long proposal helps strategy; early monetization across many stakeholders is hard. Feasible as a narrow city pilot; not as a national OS soon. Weaker feasibility than single-sided SaaS.

### Why this score
**2.5/5** — Documented vision; severe multi-sided adoption barrier.

---

## 3. Impact & Relevance — 4.5/5

### What was evaluated
Emergency mortality/morbidity relevance in Bangladesh.

### Analysis
Ambulance delay, triage confusion, and family notification failures are high-stakes. If coordination works, lives-saved potential is large. Score reflects problem severity more than proven deployment.

### Why this score
**4.5/5** — Top-tier problem relevance; impact contingent on overcoming cold-start.
